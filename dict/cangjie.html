新説解　仓頡字根

A superscripted number next to a letter indicates a subcomponent (輔助字形). These are only used to sort the first letters of Cangjie codes and are omitted in lower-order sorting. That is to say, all indices zn are reduced to z except when representing the head component of a Chinese character.

Also note that the combining radical 礻 is reclassed under Y1 to avoid a double allography.

Collision Resolution

Where there are collisions of character codes, order them by the specific auxiliary component as defined in this chart (for example, 日 before 曰, 人 before 入, and so on). If every such component is identical, place an X (难) after the less common character. Dictionaries may use a frequency chart for this information, but it is abstracted away from the user, who may need to scan a few entries to obtain the desired lookup.
