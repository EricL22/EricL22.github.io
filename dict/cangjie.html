新説解　仓頡字根

A superscripted number next to a letter indicates a subcomponent (輔助字形). These are only used to sort the first letters of Cangjie codes and are omitted in lower-order sorting. That is to say all indices zn are reduced to z except when representing the head component of a Chinese character.

Collision Resolution

Within the most common characters, collisions are virtually nonexistent. However, extending the system across all Chinese characters will invariably generate a collision of character codes. In such cases, order them by the specific auxiliary component as defined in this chart (for example, 日 before 曰, and so on).
