Radical Order

This dictionary makes use of a novel indexing technique known as head-tail collation (頭尾整理). In it, each radical is treated as a letter of an "alphabet," and each character decomposes into a permutation of radicals from top to bottom, left to right. There may exist groups of strokes that cannot be classified as radicals; these are each represented by "difficult" (难). Code collisions are resolved by order of frequency.

To simplify and adapt this cumbersome listing to this new era of unified writing, some traditional radicals were removed:

  • 5 Strokes
    • 龙: merged with 尢, 3 strokes.
  • 6 Strokes
    • 齐: merged with 文, 4 strokes.
  • 7 Strokes
    • 里: merged with 田, 5 strokes.
  • 17 Strokes
    • 龠: merged with 人, 2 strokes.

Input Method

As shown above, the 210 radicals may be divided into 24 groups for the purposes of input. The groups formed are arbitrary to preserve the original Kangxi ordering, but there are still divisions depending on stroke count; hence A represents radicals with the smallest stroke count (1) and Y the largest (14). X and Z exist as control keys for difficult and repeated codes, respectively, taking after the Canjie input method.
