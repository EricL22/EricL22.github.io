The sokuon kana is represented by the doubling of the following consonant in its syllable; in particular, ㅋ (k) becomes ㄲ (kk), ㅌ (t) becomes ㄸ (tt), and ㅊ (ch) becomes ㅉ (tch).

Long vowels are represented differently depending on the lengthened vowel. If it is reflected in the kana orthography as a doubling of the vowel, it is represented as a chōonpu, otherwise, it is represented as a final ㅎ:

Romaji Hangeul
hā (haa) 하ー
hī (hii) 히ー
fū (fuu) ᅗᅳー
hē (hee or hei) 헤ー or 헿
hō (hoo or hou) 호ー or 홓

Also note that for nonstandard lengthening of vowels (beyond one mora), only the chōonpu is used with multiple instances, each instance representing the length of one mora.
